We've all been there — striving harder, doing more, quietly convinced that if we just get it right enough, God will be pleased. But what if the striving itself is the problem?

In The Self-Righteous Saint, Danielle Niyotwagira traces her own journey from performance-driven faith to the radical rest of abiding — and invites readers to make the same courageous shift.

"I do not set aside the grace of God; for if righteousness comes through the law, then Christ died in vain."

Galatians 2:21

In her own words

My most important realization in the past 5 years is that the most dangerous aspect of self-righteousness is how easily it masquerades as spiritual maturity. When you're convinced that your motives are pure, your theology is sound, and your heart is right before God, it becomes almost impossible to recognize the ways that pride has infected your faith. You can quote verses about humility while looking down on others. You can preach about grace while keeping a mental scorecard of who's really living up to biblical standards. You can sing about God's love while harboring criticism and judgment toward fellow believers who don't measure up to your expectations.

This book is my attempt to shine a light on this particular form of spiritual blindness. Not from a position of having arrived at perfect humility, but from the perspective of someone who is still learning what it means to live in the freedom and grace given through our Lord Jesus Christ. It's written for anyone who has ever struggled with the tension between knowing the right answers and living them out with genuine love and humility.

About the Author

You are probably visiting this page because you'd like to learn a little more about me.

My name is Danielle Nicole Niyotwagira. I am a wife, a mother to two wonderful little girls, and a teacher specializing in special education.

Over the years, I have learned that you do not need to be fearless or have a perfect plan to move forward. You simply have to decide that where God is leading you is greater than the fear trying to hold you back.

My hope is that this book sparks something within you and helps you allow Christ to transform the way you understand His grace — reminding you that His grace truly is enough.
